Jim Henson, the Man behind the Muppet

man or muppet?

Jim Henson was born on September 24, 1936 in Greenville, Mississippi, and passed away on May 16, 1990 full name James Maury Henson, was an American puppeteer, animator, cartoonist, actor, inventor, and filmmaker who went on to create the Muppets. The Muppets were a group of diverse characters including Kermit the Frog, Miss Piggy, Fozzie Bear, and Gonzo. His puppetry took off as an early teenager, starring on local television shows, and TV commercials. In my opinion, Jim Henson is an amazing role model and all around a creative genius all packed into one aingle human.

Puppetering! Jim Henson's Endeavors

With a new reputation behind him, Jim Henson takes on many commercials, starring in the albeit sadistic, Wilkinson Coffee, videos for IBM, dog food commercials (here, Rolfe the Dog, the first nationally recognized Muppet, debuts) and the biggest one ever: Sesame Street in 1969. Children's Television Workshop contacted Jim Henson in need of muppets for their new television show, leading Henson to create a cast of the most notable muppets still on the show such as Bert and Ernie, Big Bird, Oscar the Grouch, and Grover. These characters were a big hit, as evident even after more than fifty years running, children still grow up with this show. Even though Henson found success in this endeavor, he himself didn't have much luck in starting his own show. American shows didn't show much interest in a new cast of muppets, however, Jim ended up being funded by a British independent producer named Lew Grade. This is where The Muppets Show was born.
